The Science of Cannabis

Understanding the Endocannabinoid System and Qualifying Conditions.

How It Works: The Endocannabinoid System

The Endocannabinoid System (ECS) is a complex cell-signaling system identified in the early 1990s. It plays a crucial role in regulating a range of functions and processes, including sleep, mood, appetite, memory, and pain. Its primary goal is homeostasisâ€”maintaining the body's internal balance.

Figure 1: Distribution of CB1 (Central Nervous System) and CB2 (Immune System) receptors.

CB1 Receptors

Found primarily in the brain and central nervous system. They influence pain, mood, and motor control. THC binds directly here.

CB2 Receptors

Found primarily in the peripheral nervous system and immune cells. They influence inflammation and pain response.

Qualifying Conditions

While the FDA has not approved the whole cannabis plant for medical use, many states have authorized its use for specific, debilitating conditions. Common qualifying conditions include:

Category Condition Potential Benefit
Chronic Pain Neuropathy, Arthritis, Severe Injury Reduction of inflammation and modulation of pain signals.
Neurological Epilepsy, Multiple Sclerosis (MS), Parkinson's Reduction in seizure frequency and muscle spasticity.
Gastrointestinal Crohn's, Ulcerative Colitis, Nausea Stimulation of appetite and reduction of nausea (especially Chemo-induced).
Mental Health PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder) Management of anxiety, intrusive thoughts, and sleep disturbances.

*Note: Always verify with your specific state's Department of Health list.

The State of Research

Research into the therapeutic benefits of cannabis is ongoing but hindered by federal regulations. However, specific cannabinoid drugs have received FDA approval:

  • Epidiolex (CBD): Approved for treating seizures associated with Lennox-Gastaut and Dravet syndromes.
  • Marinol (Dronabinol): Synthetic THC approved for nausea caused by chemotherapy.
